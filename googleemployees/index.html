From Google employees to Google policy. Why aren't you standing up for the Open Internet?

Google employees and executives overwhelmingly support real net neutrality, so why are Google’s Republican lobbyists in DC standing in the way of Google supporting real net neutrality (Title II)?

Dear Mountain View Google:

Google helped pioneer open Internet principles, but Google's been notably absent from the net neutrality debate. Despite overwhelming support from Google employees and execs for real net neutrality, Republican lobbyists have kept Google from playing an important role in this debate. As Google employees, we're writing to urge our Google execs to not be silent anymore, and stand with real net neutrality.

From,

Google employees

Why We Care

Even though 3.7 millions comments have been filed overwhelmingly in favor of net neutrality, Google filed no comments. Google has simply hidden behind some trade associations and wished the issue would just go away. The company has given up its leadership in fighting for an open Internet. But the future of the Internet is at stake. So its employees must stand up, together with the millions of Americans, hundreds of startups, and thousands of small businesses that are fighting the giant cable and phone monopolies on their own.

As Google employees, we chose to work for the Open Internet. Today, we need to choose the Open Internet loud and clear; we want our leadership to stand up for net neutrality and urge reclassification of ISP’s as telecommunications services.

As Google employees, we are proud to work for a company that has become synonymous with the principles of the open Internet.

The debate over net neutrality is one we can not stay out of. Google has signed onto a letter for net neutrality, but at this point we must do more.

We can’t allow for a new era of American discrimination. We can’t have the Internet of Europe where many fewer companies start up and thrive.

The Internet has to be a bastion of creativity and new ideas. We must do all we can to keep the US Internet a leading example of free speech and open innovation.

We must stand with the most legally defensible way to achieve net neutrality, that is reclassification of ISP’s as Title II common carriers.

We believe in the power of the Internet and we must also recognize our power in this debate. We urge Google leadership to stand with us and take a stand now to ensure net neutrality.
